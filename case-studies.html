Case 1 — Boutique Ontario law firm

The setup. 12-person commercial-litigation firm with a backlog of NDA and vendor-MSA reviews. They'd been trying to hire a junior paralegal for 8 months and couldn't find one in their budget ($65–75K). Partners were doing first-pass redlines after hours.

What they did. Hired David (Paralegal) at $1,997/mo on the Growth package. Connected him to their Clio Manage repository and DocuSign CLM. Set a rule: David flags, partners decide.

What changed. NDA turnaround dropped from 36 hours to 4. Average MSA review time fell from 4 hours of partner time to 25 minutes of partner review on David's draft. Partners reclaimed roughly 6 billable hours each per week (~$1,800/wk in additional billings per partner). The paralegal job posting was withdrawn — the firm now plans to hire a senior associate instead, using the recovered margin.

The partner-friendly part. Their external recruiter still gets the senior-associate search. Their managing CPA still does year-end. Clio's contract is unchanged. Nobody got squeezed.

Case 2 — Multi-clinic dental group (BC)

The setup. Four-location group with a shared central reception team. Missed evening and weekend calls were costing them roughly 15 new-patient bookings per month. Front desk was burning out on recall outreach.

What they did. Hired Maya (Receptionist) at the Growth tier. Connected her to Jane App across all four clinics. Maya handles every call between 5pm and 8am, every weekend, plus all proactive recall outreach.

What changed. Missed-call rate dropped to zero. New-patient bookings up 22% inside 90 days. Front-desk team stayed intact — they now handle the in-person experience and complex cases, which they prefer. Patient satisfaction (post-visit survey) up 11 points.

The partner-friendly part. Jane App is their PMS partner — Maya operates inside it, doesn't replace it. The dental staffing agency they use for hygienists still does their thing. The dentists practice dentistry.

Case 3 — Regional CPA partner firm

The setup. 28-person accounting firm serving small-business clients. Their junior bookkeepers were spending 70% of their time on transaction categorization and reconciliation — the work nobody wants to do. The firm was losing junior staff faster than they could train them.

What they did. Deployed Quinn (Bookkeeper) across 40+ active client QuickBooks Online files. The arrangement: Quinn does the daily reconciliation; junior bookkeepers review Quinn's work and handle exceptions; senior accountants do tax, advisory, and CFO services.

What changed. The firm took on 14 new SMB clients in 9 months without a single new hire. Junior bookkeeper retention improved (the work is now interesting). Margin per client went up ~18%. The firm now markets "AI-assisted bookkeeping" as a tier of service.

The partner-friendly part. They're a QuickBooks ProAdvisor partner. Quinn makes that partnership more valuable, not less. Intuit's product stays at the center.

Case 4 — Toronto vertical-SaaS startup

The setup. 22-person Series A SaaS company. Two AEs, zero SDRs. Founders were doing prospecting between meetings. Sales cycle was lumpy because pipeline kept going dry.

What they did. Hired Sam (SDR) at the Growth tier. Connected to HubSpot, Apollo, Clay, LinkedIn Sales Navigator. Built ICP rules with the AEs. Sam sources, enriches, sequences, and books — AEs do discovery and close.

What changed. Booked meetings up 3.1× in 90 days. Pipeline value up 2.4×. AE ramp time for a new hire cut roughly in half (because the pipeline is now consistent). The 2026 plan was to hire two SDRs at ~$75K each — that hire is now deferred indefinitely.

The partner-friendly part. They use a fractional VP Sales coach. Sam doesn't compete with the coach — Sam executes; the coach strategizes. HubSpot is their CRM forever.

Case 5 — Calgary managed-services provider

The setup. 18-person MSP supporting 200+ SMB clients. Tier-1 ticket volume was crushing the help desk overnight. After-hours coverage was costing them $8K/mo in on-call premium.

What they did. Deployed Theo (Community + Support) into their Zendesk instance. Theo handles tier-1 tickets autonomously between 6pm and 8am — password resets, common config questions, ticket routing. Escalates anything ambiguous to the on-call human.

What changed. First-response time dropped from 4 hours to 6 minutes overnight. CSAT up 14 points. On-call premium cut to $2K/mo (humans only paged for true escalations). Tier-1 ticket volume hitting humans down 71%.

The partner-friendly part. Their PSA vendor (ConnectWise) stays. Their RMM tool stays. Theo just operates inside the tools they already pay for.

Case 6 — Quebec professional-services rollup

The setup. 60-person PE-backed rollup with 7 senior partners across two Quebec offices. Data residency was a hard requirement (Law 25). Partners were drowning in email and calendar coordination.

What they did. Deployed Olivia (EA) on the Sovereign tier — dedicated Canadian Azure tenant, customer-managed keys, 7-year immutable audit log. Each partner gets their own Olivia instance, trained on their voice and priorities.

What changed. Each partner reclaimed ~9 hours/week. Partner-utilization rate up 12 points. Email response SLA went from 24 hours to 2. The CFO's exact quote: "the cheapest senior hire we've made."

The partner-friendly part. They have a longstanding outsourced CIO. The CIO approved the Sovereign architecture and remains the security owner. Wilfred reports to him.
